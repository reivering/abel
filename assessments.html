Final Exam Question Packet ▼
Knowledge [K]

Problem: Define the derivative using limits.

Worked Solution:
The limit definition of the derivative describes the instantaneous rate of change of a function \( f(x) \) at a given point by taking secant lines getting closer together.

\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \)
Application [A]

Problem: Compute the derivative of \( f(x) = x^3 e^x \).

Worked Solution:
Step 1: Identify product rule pieces. Let \( u = x^3 \) and \( v = e^x \).
Step 2: Compute derivatives: \( u' = 3x^2 \), \( v' = e^x \).
Step 3: Apply Product Rule: \( u'v + uv' \).
Step 4: \( f'(x) = (3x^2)(e^x) + (x^3)(e^x) \).
Step 5: Factor out common terms: \( f'(x) = e^x \cdot x^2(3 + x) \).
Thinking [T]

Problem: A farmer has 2400 ft of fencing to fence a rectangular field that borders a straight river. He needs no fence along the river. What are the dimensions of the field that has the largest area?

Worked Solution:
Step 1: Let \( x \) be the width (sides perpendicular to river) and \( y \) be the length (parallel to river). Constraint: \( 2x + y = 2400 \).
Step 2: Maximize Area: \( A = xy \).
Step 3: Substitute \( y = 2400 - 2x \) into Area formula: \( A(x) = x(2400 - 2x) = 2400x - 2x^2 \).
Step 4: Take derivative: \( A'(x) = 2400 - 4x \).
Step 5: Set \( A'(x) = 0 \): \( 4x = 2400 \Rightarrow x = 600 \).
Step 6: Find \( y \): \( y = 2400 - 2(600) = 1200 \).

Dimensions yielding largest area are 600 ft by 1200 ft.
Communication [C]

Problem: Explain the reasoning behind why the derivative of a constant function, \( f(x) = c \), is identically 0. Provide both a graphical and algebraic justification.

Worked Solution:

Graphical Justification: The graph of \( f(x) = c \) is a horizontal line of height \( c \). The derivative represents the slope of the tangent line. Since the line is perfectly flat horizontally, its slope everywhere is identically \( 0 \), hence the derivative is \( 0 \).

Algebraic Justification: Using the limit definition of the derivative:
\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \)
Let \( f(x) = c \), this means for any input, the output is \( c \).
\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{c - c}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{0}{h} \)
Since the numerator is exactly 0 before taking the limit, the limit evaluates precisely to 0.
