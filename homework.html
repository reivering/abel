Homework 1 – Understanding Limits ▼

Questions

1. Evaluate the limit: \( \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2} \)

Step-by-step Solution:

Step 1: Direct substitution yields \( \frac{2^2 - 4}{2 - 2} = \frac{0}{0} \), an indeterminate form.
Step 2: Factor the numerator, which is a difference of squares: \( x^2 - 4 = (x - 2)(x + 2) \).
Step 3: Rewrite the limit: \( \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{(x - 2)(x + 2)}{x - 2} \).
Step 4: Cancel the common term \( (x - 2) \). Remember \( x \neq 2 \) so this is valid.
Step 5: Evaluate \( \lim_{x \to 2} (x + 2) = 2 + 2 = 4 \).

Answer: 4
Homework 2 – Derivative Rules ▼

Questions

1. Find \( f'(x) \) for \( f(x) = 3x^4 - 2x^2 + 5x - \pi \)

Step-by-step Solution:

Step 1: Apply the Power Rule: \( \frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = nx^{n-1} \).
Step 2: Derivative of \( 3x^4 \) is \( 4 \cdot 3x^3 = 12x^3 \).
Step 3: Derivative of \( -2x^2 \) is \( 2 \cdot -2x = -4x \).
Step 4: Derivative of \( 5x \) is \( 5 \).
Step 5: The derivative of a constant (\(-\pi\)) is 0.

Answer: \( f'(x) = 12x^3 - 4x + 5 \)
Homework 3 – Tangent Lines ▼

Questions

1. Find the equation of the tangent line to the curve \( y = x^2 + 3 \) at \( x = 1 \).

Step-by-step Solution:

Step 1: Find the point on the curve. At \( x = 1 \), \( y = (1)^2 + 3 = 4 \). The point is (1, 4).
Step 2: Find the derivative to determine the slope \( m \). \( y' = 2x \).
Step 3: Evaluate \( y' \) at \( x = 1 \). Slope \( m = 2(1) = 2 \).
Step 4: Use point-slope form: \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \).
Step 5: Substitute: \( y - 4 = 2(x - 1) \). Simplify to \( y = 2x - 2 + 4 \).

Answer: \( y = 2x + 2 \)
Homework 4 – Optimization Problems ▼

Questions

1. An open box is to be made from a 12x12 inch piece of cardboard by cutting equal squares from each corner and folding up the sides. What size square should be cut to maximize volume?

Step-by-step Solution:

Step 1: Let \( x \) be the side length of the square to be cut. The dimensions of the box are base: \( (12 - 2x) \times (12 - 2x) \) and height \( x \).
Step 2: Write the volume function: \( V(x) = x(12 - 2x)^2 \). Domain is \( (0, 6) \).
Step 3: Expand \( V(x) = x(144 - 48x + 4x^2) = 4x^3 - 48x^2 + 144x \).
Step 4: Find critical points where \( V'(x) = 0 \). \( V'(x) = 12x^2 - 96x + 144 \).
Step 5: Set to 0 and divide by 12: \( x^2 - 8x + 12 = 0 \). Factor: \( (x - 2)(x - 6) = 0 \).
Step 6: Since \( x=6 \) yields 0 volume, the max occurs at \( x = 2 \).

Answer: 2 inch squares.
Homework 5 – Introduction to Integrals ▼

Questions

1. Evaluate the definite integral: \( \int_{0}^{2} (3x^2 + 2x) \, dx \)

Step-by-step Solution:

Step 1: Find the antiderivative using reverse power rule: \( \int(ax^n)dx = \frac{a}{n+1}x^{n+1} \).
Step 2: Anti-derivative of \( 3x^2 \) is \( x^3 \).
Step 3: Anti-derivative of \( 2x \) is \( x^2 \). Total: \( F(x) = x^3 + x^2 \).
Step 4: Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus: \( F(2) - F(0) \).
Step 5: Evaluate: \( (2^3 + 2^2) - (0^3 + 0^2) = (8 + 4) - 0 = 12 \).

Answer: 12
